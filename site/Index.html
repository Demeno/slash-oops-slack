How it Works

FAQ

What does this app actually do?

When you send the command "/oops" the app looks for the latest message you sent in the current channel in the last 5 minutes, deletes it, and privately shows you a copy of what you wrote.

Can't I just delete messages some other way?

Yes, but "/oops" is much quicker and also gives you a copy.

Why are these app permissions needed?
"Add commands"

So you can send the "/oops" command

"Access content in your public channels"

For finding your recent message in a public channel

"Access content in your private channels"

For finding your recent message in a private channel

"Access content in your direct messages"

For finding your recent message in direct messaging

"Send messages as you"

For deleting a message on your behalf

Privacy

What personal data do we store?

We store the basic info we get from Slack when you add the app (user id, team id & team name).
We temporarily keep logs for debugging purposes, but without personal information.

We don't store the contents of any of your messages, that wouldn't be cool.

How do we protect your data?

Our application is hosted in a secure server provided by heroku.com (you can read their security policy here). We use a secure connection between Slack's servers and ours.

Do we share data with third parties?

We don't share any of the (very little) data we have with any third party.
